Identification of Architectural Requirements ()
A list of architectural requirements is compiled, based on the previous presentations. Architectural requirements include all aspects that drive the architecture, including high-level requirements, business drivers, constraints, and quality attributes. At this stage, they should be expressed in the language of the stakeholders: flexibility, performance, availability, time-to-market, compliance and so on. The concept of architectural requirements may best be explained to stakeholders by calling them "solution drivers" or "forces". Where there are gaps, the missing requirements should be identified as part of this activity. It is important that the facilitators assume a leading role in addressing these requirements, as otherwise you might end up focusing on the functional aspects. Make sure non-functional requirements are included. Find a way to obtain buy-in from all Stakeholders present, and try to achieve consensus on the list of Architectural Requirements resulting from this step.
